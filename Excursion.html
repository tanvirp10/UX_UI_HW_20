Excursion

A budget-based traveling mobile app designed to help people traveling better financially

Role
Solo UX Designer

About the Project

The objective of this project is to develop a user-friendly mobile app designed specifically for certain target audiences, with the aim of simplifying travel budget management. Traveling offers abundant opportunities to explore different cultures, pursue personal interests, and enjoy leisure activities. However, based on recent feedback, it is known that stress and time constraints are major challenges when it comes to trip planning, whether traveling alone or with others. To address these issues, I have envisioned a streamlined mobile app that simplifies the process of travel planning. With just a single link, users can effortlessly invite others who are involved in their journey and collaborate on managing the trip's budget.

Methodology

To develop a comprehensive approach, I begin by strategizing the necessary steps for creating an effective design. This entails conducting thorough user research to gain insights into their needs and preferences. Additionally, analyzing competitors allows us to identify areas where we can provide superior solutions. Engaging in brainstorming sessions enables us to generate innovative ideas that give us a competitive edge. Subsequently, we create prototypes that embody these ideas and put them to the test through user testing, ensuring that our designs meet user expectations and deliver optimal user experiences.

User Research

Interviews

To pinpoint the weak points in travel experiences and transform them into strengths through a mobile app, I conduct a series of five user interviews. These interviews serve as a valuable tool for identifying specific pain points that travelers encounter. By engaging with users directly, I gather insights into their challenges, frustrations, and areas where improvement is needed. These findings then become the foundation for enhancing the mobile app's features and functionality. The goal is to address the identified weaknesses and transform them into strengths, ultimately creating a travel app that offers seamless and satisfying experiences for users.

Competitive Analysis

After conducting user interviews, I proceed to identify my direct competitors in the market. Two notable competitors that emerge from my research are TravelSpend and Tripcoin. By studying their approaches, we can gain insights into market trends, user expectations, and areas where we can differentiate our app to provide a more advantageous and compelling solution for travelers.

Ideation & Development

Following the competitive analysis, it becomes evident that developing a collaborative travel app would be highly beneficial, considering that traveling with companions is more common than solo travel. To visualize the app's layout, I create wireframes and low-fidelity prototypes. These design representations help me determine the overall structure and appearance of the mobile app, allowing me to iterate and refine the user interface before moving on to higher-fidelity prototypes and eventual development.

User Testing

To prioritize the user's needs and preferences, I conduct user tests on the prototype I've developed. These tests involve engaging real users to gather valuable feedback and insights regarding their experience with the app. This approach allows me to identify areas where enhancements can be made, new features can be added, or existing functionalities can be refined.

High-Fidelity Prototype

After conducting user tests and incorporating the feedback received, I proceed to develop a high-fidelity prototype. By refining the design elements, incorporating visual aesthetics, and ensuring a seamless user experience, the high-fidelity prototype serves as a near-complete representation of the final product.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the entire learning experience proved to be an invaluable learning curve for me. Through user research, I gained insights into the target audience and identified key priorities for the prototype. Adopting a user-centric approach allowed me to view the product from the user's perspective, enabling me to identify areas where I could introduce unique features that differentiate my app from competitors. By considering the user's needs and incorporating their feedback, I aimed to create a polished and well-rounded final product. Overall, this process has been instrumental in shaping my understanding of designing for user satisfaction and has contributed to the development of a compelling and differentiated solution.
